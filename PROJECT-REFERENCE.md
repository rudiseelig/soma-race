## Bachelor Thesis: Human vs. AI Design Process

**Author:** Ketevani Kushashvili
**Institution:** IU Internationale Hochschule
**Program:** B.A. Mediendesign
**Supervisor:** Prof. Christina Reisinger
**Duration:** 3 months

## Research Question
Can generative AI tools independently solve visual communication tasks with the same quality, originality, and contextual understanding as a human designer?

## Methodology
Two design workflows received identical briefs. One involved fully AI-powered creation (moodboard through final visuals). The other was entirely human-created (concept through actual photoshoot). Participants compare both without initial knowledge of origin.

---

## The Brief: SOMA Branding

**Brand Concept:** A modern aphrodisiac oil — plant-based wellness product emphasizing natural sensuality and emotional reconnection.

**Deliverables:** Brand name, logo, visual identity direction, packaging mockup, moodboard, tone of voice

**Target Audience:** Ages 25–45, gender-inclusive, design-conscious, natural/mindful values, urban lifestyle

**Visual Style Keywords:** Minimalist · Earth-toned · Modern & Soft · Quiet Luxury · Organic Textures · Feminine & Masculine Balance

**Restrictions:** No florals, nothing clinical, nothing explicitly erotic

## TRACK A: Human-Centric Workflow

**Total Duration:** ~2 months (33–38 active working hours)
**Background thinking:** Ideas developed in shower, commutes, unrelated moments — diffuse thinking inseparable from the work

### 8 Stages (Chronological Order)

#### 1. Product Sourcing (4–5 hours)
- Finding a physical oil bottle to photograph and label
- Most manufacturers require MOQ of 50+ units
- Eventually found supplier in China willing to sell one unit
- 2-week delivery window before visual work could begin
- Constraint AI track doesn't have: needing a physical object

#### 2. Visual Research & Stock Image Exploration (8–9 hours)
- Building visual reference library
- Studying sensual texture, oil surfaces, warm lighting, botanical aesthetics
- Goal: develop instinct for what brand SHOULDN'T look like
- SOMA brief restrictions (no florals, nothing clinical, nothing erotic) harder to navigate than expected
- Many reference images quietly violated at least one rule

#### 3. Color Palette Development (2 hours)
- Earth tones holding sensuality + natural warmth
- Sunset lamp brought in — testing colors under physical warm lighting
- Colors that look balanced on monitor feel different on physical surface under atmospheric lighting
- Palette grounded in physical experience, not just screen-based picking

#### 4. Logo Design (~1 week)
- Longest and most uncertain stage
- Multiple dead ends with typographic approaches
- Needed: hand-touched, organic — not rustic or rough
- **BREAKTHROUGH:** Painting letters by hand on paper, then tracing with HONEY as medium
- Honey photographed with camera, scanned with phone
- Manually traced and vectorized in Adobe Illustrator
- Final logomark carries subtle imperfections of physical process
- Not simulated hand-lettering — actual physical process
- Emerged from frustration with every other direction

#### 5. Product Photography (4h shooting + 4–5h editing)
- Two cameras, mini photography box, sunset lamp
- Problem: dropper pipette didn't fit in outer packaging
- Resolved in post — composited in Photoshop
- Heavily retouched final photographs
- Stock photos from Cosmos incorporated (human-made, tonal consistency)

#### 6. Copywriting & Brand Voice (5–6 hours)
- Genuinely difficult — English not native language
- Writing sensual, refined copy without awkwardness
- Extensive external input: friends, colleagues, supervising professor
- Ongoing informal feedback rounds throughout project
- Getting tone right for intimacy brand in second language: humbling

#### 7. Website Prototype (2–3 hours)
- Layout structure and user flow focus
- Visual identity established enough by this point
- Organizing and applying existing design decisions

#### 8. Brand Book Documentation (5 hours)
- Compiling visual identity, design rationale, process outcomes
- Structured presentation format

### Resources Used
- 2 cameras
- Photography light box
- Sunset lamp
- Sticker paper for physical labels
- Olive oil
- Spare pipette
- Honey (logo medium)
- Adobe Creative Suite
- Figma
- Stock image licensing (Cosmos)
- Package shipped from China

### Key Human Qualities
- Diffuse background thinking (2 months of subconscious processing)
- Feedback loops with trusted people
- False starts and discarded directions
- Accumulated uncertainty that doesn't compress into hours log
- Physical material experimentation (honey, real lighting, real products)
- Emotional/cultural navigation (second language, sensual tone)

## TRACK B: AI-Exclusive Workflow

**Total Duration:** ~2 hours (ChatGPT) / <1 hour (Gemini)

### Systems Tested
- **ChatGPT (GPT-4o)** — main workflow, won final selection
- **Gemini Pro** — faster but lower quality
- External tools: Midjourney (image gen), Krea (refinement), Ideogram Flora (unused)

### ChatGPT Workflow

#### Problems Encountered
1. **Context retention failure:** Forgot brand name "SOMA" twice — started calling it "Velora"
2. **Content filter friction:** Midjourney blocked ~50% of prompts due to aphrodisiac/sensuality topic
3. **Raster-only output:** Logos need vectors, AI generates rasters — manual vectorization needed in Figma
4. **Requires orchestration:** More human management produced better results

#### 8 Phases (Self-organized by ChatGPT)
1. Project Analysis & Setup
2. Moodboard & Visual Language Direction
3. Color Palette & Typography
4. Logo & Symbol Design
5. Product Packaging Mockups
6. Backside Layout & Campaign Visuals
7. Messaging & Voice Definition
8. Brand Experience Mockups

#### Strengths
- Self-organized branding phases unprompted
- Moodboard direction: earthy, muted, warm (matched brief)
- Typography: Canela serif (good choice)
- Logo: drop shape with S curve (conceptually sound)
- Packaging mockups: strongest deliverables — readable text, serial numbers, QR codes, ingredient lists
- Referenced Aesop, Grown Alchemist aesthetics

### Gemini Workflow
- Better context comprehension (no name confusion)
- Used native Nano Banana image model (no external tools)
- Lower quality outputs: muddy moodboards, low resolution
- **Shadow artifact:** Uncorrectable rendering error propagated through entire identity system
- Less detail in packaging, no back-panel refinement

### Why ChatGPT Won
- Depth over speed
- Production-ready quality vs half-finished
- The system needing MORE human management produced BETTER results
- Key insight: "orchestration vs autonomy" — current AI tools aren't ready to work unsupervised

## Survey Structure

### Round 1 — Visual Quality & Aesthetics
Comparative rating of polish, professionalism, appeal (1-5 scales)

### Round 2 — Brand Coherence
Assessment of unified visual storytelling across all elements (1-5 scales)

### Round 3 — Emotional Impact
Evaluation of sensuality, authenticity, brand essence alignment (1-5 + open-ended)

### Round 4 — Verdict & Prediction
Participant preference selection + AI-origin guessing

### Post-Reveal Questions
- Does knowing origin change preference?
- Efficiency trade-offs (time/resources vs quality)
- Can AI fully replace human designers? (1-5)
- Comparative strengths (open-ended)

### Demographics
- Background: Designer, Marketing, Student, Tech, Creative, Non-creative
- Age: 18-24, 25-34, 35-44, 45+
- AI familiarity: 1-5 (never used → daily)
